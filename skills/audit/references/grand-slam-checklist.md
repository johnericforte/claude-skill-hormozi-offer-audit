# Grand Slam Offer Checklist

> Source: Alex Hormozi, _$100M Offers_ (2021). Framework cited descriptively under nominative fair use.

## Definition

A Grand Slam Offer is one so well-constructed that the prospect feels stupid saying no. It scores high on all four Value Equation levers AND uses demand-enhancement levers to raise willingness to pay without changing the core deliverable.

## Part 1: 5-Step Build (audit checklist)

For audit purposes, treat each build step as a yes/no checklist item against the existing offer.

### Step 1: Dream Outcome identified?

Does the offer name the buyer's desired result in their language?

- ✅ Pass: "Add 30 booked calls to your calendar in 60 days."
- ❌ Fail: "Comprehensive lead generation system."

### Step 2: Obstacles listed?

Does the offer acknowledge the obstacles the buyer will hit?

- ✅ Pass: "You've tried cold email. It dies after 2 weeks. You've tried ads. CAC is too high. You've tried referrals. Pipeline is lumpy."
- ❌ Fail: Offer goes straight to features without naming what the buyer has already tried and failed at.

### Step 3: Obstacles reversed into solutions?

Does each obstacle in step 2 have a corresponding solution in the bundle?

- ✅ Pass: For each named obstacle, the offer shows a specific component that addresses it.
- ❌ Fail: Obstacles named but bundle doesn't map to them. Or bundle has 12 items, only 2 of which target real obstacles.

### Step 4: Delivery vehicle chosen?

Does the offer state HOW the buyer gets the outcome?

Three primary modes:

- **Done-for-you (DFY):** highest price, lowest buyer effort
- **Done-with-you (DWY):** mid price, moderate effort, includes guidance
- **Do-it-yourself (DIY):** lowest price, highest effort

- ✅ Pass: One mode is clearly named and the page describes what's included.
- ❌ Fail: Vague delivery ("our system", "the platform") that doesn't tell the buyer what THEY have to do.

### Step 5: Stack trimmed?

Has the offer removed low-value/high-cost items and kept high-value ones?

- ✅ Pass: Each component in the bundle has a stated value, fits the Dream Outcome, and serves a real obstacle.
- ❌ Fail: Bundle padded with bonuses that don't connect to the outcome ("plus a free meditation app!").

## Part 2: 5 Enhancement Levers (raise demand without changing core offer)

These multiply the perceived value of the existing bundle.

### Scarcity

Limited supply. Real, not fake.

- "10 seats per cohort"
- "5 spots open this month"
- "Only 100 copies of the print edition"

Fake scarcity ("only 3 left" that resets daily) destroys trust. Audit flag: if the scarcity claim isn't auditable, score it weak.

### Urgency

Limited time. Real deadline tied to a real event.

- "Cohort starts March 15"
- "Price goes up to $1,500 on Friday"
- "Bonus expires when the next 50 buyers sign up"

Audit flag: countdown timers without a real reason behind them = weak.

### Bonuses

Additional items stacked into the offer with NAMED VALUES. Each bonus should:

- Solve a specific obstacle from step 2
- Have a stated standalone value
- Total bonus stack value > price of the offer

Audit flag: bonuses without stated values = weak. Bonuses unrelated to the Dream Outcome = drop them.

### Guarantees

Risk reversal. Three flavors:

- **Money-back** ("30-day refund, no questions asked") — easiest, lowest signal
- **Performance** ("you don't get X, we refund Y") — strong, ties to outcome
- **Anti-guarantee** ("we don't refund. Here's why we're confident anyway.") — counter-intuitive, works for high-ticket

Audit flag: no guarantee = weak. Vague guarantee ("satisfaction guaranteed") = weak.

### Naming (M.A.G.I.C.)

A magnetic offer name compresses the entire pitch into one phrase. Five components:

- **M**agnetic reason — why the buyer should care RIGHT NOW
- **A**nnounce avatar — who the offer is for
- **G**ive them a goal — the Dream Outcome
- **I**ndicate timeframe — when they get it
- **C**ontainer word — the noun that wraps it (Program, System, Method, Blueprint, Accelerator, Intensive)

Example: "The 6-Week Lead-Gen Accelerator for B2B SaaS Founders" hits all 5.

Audit flag: most published offers fail M.A.G.I.C. The name is "[Company] Pro" or "[Product] Premium". This is the most-overlooked lever.

## Audit output format

For each item above:

```
Step 1 (Dream Outcome): PASS — "Cut churn by 40% in 90 days" is named.
Step 2 (Obstacles):     FAIL — copy goes from headline to features, no obstacle naming.
Step 3 (Reversals):     N/A — depends on Step 2.
Step 4 (Delivery):      PASS — DFY clearly stated.
Step 5 (Stack):         WEAK — 14 bonuses listed, only 4 tied to Dream Outcome.
Scarcity:               NOT PRESENT
Urgency:                NOT PRESENT
Bonuses:                WEAK (4 bonuses, no stated values)
Guarantees:             PRESENT (30-day money-back, weak signal)
Naming:                 FAIL (M.A.G.I.C. score 1/5 — only Container word)
```
